Safety First

We keep it exciting — and structured.

  • Small groups for better control and service
  • Clear rules and a practical briefing
  • Visibility buoys for every guest
  • Routes chosen for manageable boat traffic and surface conditions
  • Weather-dependent planning (we don't force bad days)

Requirements

To get the most out of your adventure, we ask that you:

  • Can swim unaided for at least 50 metres in open water
  • Are aged 10 or over — younger adventurers considered on a case-by-case basis, just reach out first
  • Feel comfortable wearing a mask and snorkel at the surface
  • Have no significant medical conditions that could present a risk in open water
  • Arrive free from alcohol or any impairing substances

If you're unsure, message us — we'll help you decide quickly.

FAQ

Quick answers.

Do I need scuba experience?

No. This is a surface snorkelling experience — no diving involved. Some comfort in the water is needed, but no certifications or prior experience required.

Will I go deep?

No — this stays shallow and controlled, with swim buoys to support safe limits. But the ocean is still the ocean, so a basic level of comfort in open water is important.

What if the weather is bad?

Safety first: the trip is weather-dependent. If conditions aren't right, we'll reschedule or apply the policy shown at booking.

Can I come if my partner is diving?

Definitely yes — this makes it a great option for non-diving partners who still want a real ocean experience.

What should I bring?

Swimwear, towel, sun protection, and water. We provide all the gear you need. If you own a rashguard or snorkelling suit (wetsuit) or have your own mask and fins, feel free to bring them.
